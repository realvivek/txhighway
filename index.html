about tx highway

Both roads show real transactions, live — nothing is simulated or replayed. Bitcoin vehicles enter the moment a transaction hits the public mempool; Ethereum vehicles enter as pending transactions are picked up from the network, enriched with their true value and gas price. When a block is mined, the gate at the end of the road flashes.

To keep the road readable at 30+ tx/s, small transactions carpool: one van with a ×12 badge is twelve real transactions. The pooling threshold starts at $1K and rises automatically in heavy traffic ($10K, then $100K), easing back when the road clears — the rate board below always shows the live tier. The "tx per second" sparkline in each panel shows the true arrival rate.

Lanes are the fee market: overpaying transactions take the top express lanes and drive faster, low-fee ones crawl along the bottom, everyone else fills the middle.

bigger vehicle = more value — the rate board under the highway is the scale. the golden ring marks a $1M+ whale rig, overpaying transactions drive faster, the orange-striped road carries Bitcoin, blue carries Ethereum.

The LED ticker on each gantry rotates through fee trend vs an hour ago, congestion, and the network's 24h transaction total — and on Ethereum, how fast the Base L2 side road is moving compared to the L1 highway (L2 transactions are counted, not drawn, so the road stays honest to L1). A $1M+ whale entering flashes the board.

data sources (all free & keyless)

  • blockchain.com WebSocket — live unconfirmed BTC transactions
  • mempool.space WebSocket — BTC blocks, mempool, fees
  • PublicNode / dRPC WebSocket — ETH pending txs & new heads
  • Base via PublicNode WebSocket — L2 side-road throughput
  • blockchain.com charts — 24h BTC tx total (ETH total is estimated from sampled blocks)
  • Coinbase spot API — USD prices
